E Lyricorum Libro I

"Thomae Mori constantia"

  1. Hic ille MORUS, quo melius nihil
  2. Titan* Britanno vidit ab aethere,
  3. Funesta cum Regem Bolena
  4. Illicito furiasset aestu:
  5. Audax iniquas spernere nuptias
  6. Amore veri, propositum minis
  7. Obvertit Henrici, tyranno
  8. Fortior, indocilisque flecti.
  9. Non carcer illum, non Aloysia,
  10. Dimovit uxor: nec trepidus gener*,
  11. Nec ante Patrem Margarita
  12. Faemineo lacrymosa questu.
  13. Fertur monentem mitia Conjugem,
  14. Sed non & isto digna viro, procul
  15. Abs se remotam, cum feroci,
  16. Vt fatuam*, pepulisse risu.
  17. Mox, qua flumentem se Thamesis rotat,
  18. Ad destinatum funeribus locum,
  19. Casto coronandus triumpho,
  20. Per medios properavit Anglos.
  21. Ductum secuta flente Britannia,
  22. Non flevit unus; marmore durior,
  23. Et certa despectante vultu
  24. Fata tuens*, hilarisque torvum.
  25. Atqui sciebat, quid sibi regius
  26. Tortor parasset. Non aliter tamen
  27. Quam laureatos Sulla Fasceis,
  28. Ipse suam petiit securim.
  29. Plenus futuri quo tumulo* stetit,
  30. Postquam paventem Carnificis manum
  31. Mercede firmasset, cruento
  32. Colla dedit ferienda ferro.

Ode 1.3

"The Steadfastness of Thomas More"

  1. Here lies that MORE, than whom the Titan saw
  2. Not one thing better from the British air,
  3. When dread Boleyn had woke a fury in
  4. The King with an illicit heat:
  5. He, made bold by a love of truth to spurn
  6. The sinful marriage, blocked the thing proposed
  7. By Henry’s threats, being more steadfast than
  8. The king, not knowing how to turn.
  9. Not prison nor his wife Alice moved him
  10. Off course: and not his trembling son-in-law,
  11. Nor Margaret with a woman’s pleading sobs
  12. And tears in front of her father.
  13. They say his wife who urged a gentler course,
  14. But not befitting such a man, he pushed
  15. Aside, as though a fool, with a fierce laugh
  16. And moved her back far from himself.
  17. Soon, where Thames itself its sweeping bends,
  18. Toward the place appointed for his death,
  19. So that he might be crowned with a chaste triumph,
  20. He hastened through the English mob.
  21. Led to the place as Britain followed weeping,
  22. He alone did not weep but hard as marble,
  23. With face fixed on the ground, he kept his eye
  24. On his sure fate, grim-faced but glad.
  25. Oh yes, he knew what the king’s torturer
  26. Prepared for him. But in the self-same way
  27. As Sulla did the laurel-wreathed fasces,
  28. He strove himself for his own axe.
  29. Full of the future, where for the tomb he stood,
  30. And having calmed the hangman’s shaking hand
  31. With his reward, he gave his neck
  32. To be struck by the bloody iron.
